Neighbourhood

On the edge of the Jordaan.

The apartment sits just west of the canal belt, close to the Jordaan, Haarlemmerdijk, Westerpark and the historic centre. It is a central location, but not one of the streets where Amsterdam feels most touristic.

Local character

From working-class quarter to Amsterdam icon.

The Jordaan was developed in the early seventeenth century, just outside the grand canal belt. It was not built as a merchant district, but as a dense working area of craftsmen, labourers, small houses, workshops and later immigrant communities. That history still matters. Behind today’s galleries, cafés and restored façades, the Jordaan has a more layered past: poverty, overcrowding, music, markets, local pride and repeated attempts to protect the neighbourhood from demolition or over-sanitised redevelopment.

Canals & walks

Walk east into the Jordaan and towards Prinsengracht, Westerkerk and the canal ring, or continue through the smaller streets for a quieter view of the city.

Markets

Noordermarkt is close enough for a Saturday morning walk: organic produce, flowers, bread, cheese and the kind of Amsterdam weekend rhythm that is better experienced than scheduled.

Green space

Westerpark is close by for walks, coffee, cinema, markets and open space — useful when you want a break from canals and narrow streets.
