The Jordaan Neighborhood

Central Amsterdam, canal ring edge

Location & Transport

The apartment sits on the border between the Jordaan and the canal ring, directly facing Marnixplantsoen (a green square) and Lijnbansgracht canal. It is one of Amsterdam's most walkable, tree-lined, and human-scaled neighborhoods.

Getting Around

  • Direct access: Bus 13, 18, 21 stop directly in front of the building entrance
  • Nearby tram/bus: Tram 5, bus 22 a 2-minute walk away
  • Centraal Station: 4 tram stops away (tram 5 or 13)
  • Dam Square: 20-minute walk; the heart of central Amsterdam
  • Bike-friendly: Flat terrain, bike lanes on most streets, rental shops everywhere

Nearby Markets & Shops

  • Westerstraat market: Famous local market; runs along Westerstraat, a 3-minute walk
  • Albert Cuyp Market: Large street market in the De Pijp neighborhood (10-minute walk)
  • Independent shops: The Jordaan is known for small boutiques, galleries, vintage shops, and design studios on narrow streets
  • Supermarkets: Albert Heijn and Jumbo nearby; several smaller grocery stores

Cafés & Restaurants

The neighborhood is filled with casual cafés, traditional "brown cafés" (bruine kroegen), and small restaurants. Westerstraat and Prinsengracht both have many options within walking distance.

Culture & Recreation

  • Museums: Rijksmuseum (15 min walk), Van Gogh Museum (10 min walk), Anne Frank House (nearby)
  • Parks & green space: Westerpark (10 min walk), canal-side walks
  • Galleries & studios: The Jordaan is an artistic hub; many galleries and artist studios

The Street

Marnixplein (the area immediately outside the apartment) is quiet, tree-lined, and primarily residential. You'll find a mix of local residents, bike traffic, and a few neighborhood cafés. This is "real Amsterdam" — not touristy, but lively and walkable.
